About

Two ways of seeing a problem, one practice.

I graduated from Macalester College with a computer science degree and a psychology background, a pairing that turned out to be the whole point. The CS side tells me how a system actually behaves; the psychology side tells me how a person will actually feel using it. UX design is where I get to use both at once: understanding not just how systems work, but how people think, feel, and interact with technology.

In practice, that means I'm equally comfortable running a heuristic evaluation, sketching a paper prototype, sitting in on a usability test, or picking up the front-end code to help ship the thing we designed. That range is exactly what these case studies walk through.

How I work

CliftonStrengths, top five

A Gallup CliftonStrengths assessment groups people's natural talents into themes. Mine skew toward building on what already exists and doing it precisely: useful instincts for iterative design work.

01

Developer

I notice small signs of progress and get real satisfaction from cultivating potential in others, a good match for coaching a prototype through iteration after iteration.

02

Relator

I do my best work in close, trusted collaboration, which shows up in how much of this portfolio is team-built rather than solo.

03

Consistency

I want everyone treated fairly, with clear, repeatable rules and procedures: the instinct behind wanting every page of a design to share the same logic.

04

Discipline

I default to routine and structure, and I'm at my best when a process has real order to it.

05

Analytical

I look for the reasons and causes behind a result, and I want to understand every factor that could be affecting a situation before I trust it.

Working style

A DISC assessment places me as an "Editor" profile: methodical, structured, and thorough, with a strong preference for having all the information before making a call.

Traits that come up most: meticulous, calm, purposeful. In practice, that means I'd rather improve an existing document than start a new one, I read the instructions before I start, and I pay close attention to detail, even when it's slower going.
